Steve Jobs

Steve Paul Jobs was an American inventor, entrepeneur and tycoon on computing sector. Known as the co-founder, president and CEO of Apple Inc. and for revolutionizing the industries of personal computers, animated films, music, phones, tablets and digital publishing.

Early Life and Education

Born in San Francisco (February 24, 1995), son of Joanne Schieble Jandali Simpson and Abdulfattah Jandali, and then adopted by Paul Reinhold Jobs and Clara Hagopian Jobs, who named him Steven Paul Jobs. Growed up in Palo Alto - CA, where he had the first contact with the eletronics field.
On 1972's summer, when he was 17, Steve ingresses on Reed College univerity, where he would attend for only 6 months. Nonetheless, Steve gained permission to follow the classes as an observer. Interested on a calligraphy course that years later would influence Macintosh typography.
In 1974, he gets a job at Atari and in the next year, with his high school friend Wozniak, he becomes the responsible for a version of the one-player game "Pong".
In 1978, he have a child, a girl called Lisa, who he do not asume at first and take some years to accept the parenthood. At the end of the 80's he fell in love and married with Laurene with whom he had 3 children.

Career Highlights

  • In 1976, the Apple Computer Inc is created and the first personal computer prototype called Apple I is sold.
  • In 1977, the Apple II is officially launched at the 1st West Coast Computer Faire in San Francisco, where Jobs meets a japanese fabric manufacturer whom became the first Apples' dealer on Japan.
  • In 1984, the Macintosh is released and the success of the Mac contributed to Job's increased influence over Apple who was increasingly demanding and extravagant, wearing down the teams, which would contribute to his departure from the company in the following year.
  • In 1997, Steve returns to Apple right in time to save the company from close the doors.
  • From 2000 on, the Apple released the Mac OS X, the iPod, iTunes and iPhone, increasing the sellings and allowing the inovation of the company.

Rivalries

The rivalry between Steve Jobs and Bill Gates is known as a cultural element of the sector.
Steve Jobs' relationship with Bill Gates was somewhat balanced on rivalry and friendship. Both signed multi-million-dollar deals as in the case that shortly after Jobs returned to Apple, he agreed with Microsoft to create a Microsoft Office suite for Mac. And when Apple, after the launch of the world's most famous music player, the iPod, made its advanced music service, iTunes, available to the Microsoft operating system.
In the authorized biography of Jobs, Walter Isaacson recounts an encounter between Steve and Bill. Steve Jobs was too weak to get out of bed, due to the cancer that was belatedly treated, when he received a visit from Bill Gates. Both spent a lot of time talking about their families and their lives. The visit took place shortly before the death of the Apple co-founder in 2011.

Steve passed away in Octuber 5, 2011, when he was 56 years old, due to a pancreatic cancer.
He had a huge impact on the lives of everyone who appreciates technology.
His legacy will never be forgotten.
